About Me

Everything that makes me...

  • Wanderlust:

    I love to travel

    I have been traveling since before I can remember. My parents instilled in me a love of exploring and experiencing the world. I have traveled to 43 US States (lived in 7 of them) and 13 countries (lived in 3 of them)! Now that I have my own family, we live for getting out and seeing everything the world has to offer!

  • Photography:

    An eye for scenery

    One of the advantages of growing up and travelling during the Digital Revolution, has been the ability to use so many forms of technology in so many places. I received my first film camera when I was 5 years old and haven't stopped taking pictures since then. My main subjects are landscapes and architecture. All pictures on this site are my own.

  • Event DJ:

    Passion for music and movement

    I have always been a fan of all genres of music and have been collecting albums since CDs were first introduced. Once I had a respectable collection, I began making mixtapes for friends and playing mixes at school events and house parties. Since then I have gone all digital and have been DJing weddings, bars, and other events for more than a decade.

  • Involvement:

    Helping where I can.

    Beginning with mission trips in high school, to remote, impoverished areas of Latin America, helping those less fortunate than myself is in my core. Since then I have done peacekeeping and humanitarian aid missions with the US Army, as well as worked as a mentor in a wide array of settings. One of my drives in the UX field is understanding the needs and helping those in underserved and less fortunate areas.

    going!

Civic UX Research & Crisis Management

Conducted quantitative and qualitative research to develop a solution to address local opiod epidemic.

Worked as part of a multi-disciplinary graduate project at the University of Michigan, with the Ferndale, Michigan Police Department to develop ways to address the opioid crisis in the area. While on the project, conducted user research and stakeholder interviews within the community, in order to develop an analysis of the situation and potential ways to make an impact. The end result was development of an "Opioid Harm Reduction Kit" which are staged in strategic points throughout the community to help those in need.

  • Date: Winter 2019
  • Client: Ferndale MI, Police Department
  • Category: UX Research

Community Research and Profile

Conducted quantitative and qualitative research to develop a community profile for the local public library.

Conducted user research and stakeholder interviews on behalf of Houghton Lake Public Library in order to develop a SWOT analysis of the community and the greater Roscommon County area. The desired end-state of the library was to better understand the people that they serve and how they can better help them. After analyzing data acquired through qualitative and quantitative research, I developed a report of the community and recommendations of how the library can be more engaged and directly address the needs of their populace.

  • Date: Fall 2018
  • Client: Houghton Lake Public Library
  • Category: UX Research

Campus Information Center Event Website

Conduct UX research to determine cause of low website traffic.

As part of a graduate student group project, I worked with the University of Michigan Campus Information Center to determine the cause of low student/user interest pertaining to their interactive online event calendar. We started by interviewing the client, and then conducted user testing and recording feedback. After we collected the data from observing the users and asking critical questions, we constructed an affinity wall to identify the key pain points of the website. After identifying the main causes of low website traffic, we compiled a report and presented our findings to the client.

  • Date: December 2017
  • Client: University of Michigan Campus Information Center
  • Category: UX Research

Government Website Accessibility

ADA Compliance as part of government website update.

While working for Lochbridge, we contracted with the State of Ohio to redo their Secretary of State website and make it ADA compliant. I was involved in two stages of this project. The first stage was testing all of the individual webpages and associated PDF forms to insure that they were fully ADA compliant for Accessibility. The second stage was to begin correcting minor violations on pages through HTML coding and through forms with the PDF layout. This also included fixing and repairing government forms in both English and Spanish.

While I was responsible for both of these phases, my main contribution was working directly with Adobe to understand how to utilize Acrobat Pro to recreate PDF forms in order to be fully accessible.

  • Date: February 2016
  • Client: Ohio Secretary of State
  • Category: Website Accessibility
